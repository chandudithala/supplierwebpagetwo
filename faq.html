Incoterms

What does FOB mean?

FOB stands for ‘Free on Board’. This term means that the seller delivers when the goods pass the ship's rail at the named port of shipment. The buyer has to bear all the costs and risks to the goods from that point onwards. The seller must clear the goods for export. This term can only be used for sea transport, if the parties do not intend to deliver the goods across the ship's rail, the FCA term should be used.

What does FCA mean?

The seller gives the goods, cleared for export, to the buyer’s carrier at a specified place. The buyer is then responsible for getting transported to the specified place of final delivery. This term is commonly used for containers travelling by more than one mode of transport.
